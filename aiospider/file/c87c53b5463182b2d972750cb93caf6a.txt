Types of MOT training

There are 2 types of MOT training you can provide:


  MOT tester and manager qualification courses
  MOT tester annual training courses


You must be approved by an awarding organisation to provide qualification courses.

You don’t need to be approved to provide annual training courses.

Provide qualification courses

You can get approval to provide these MOT qualifications:


  Level 2 Award in MOT Testing (classes 1 and 2 - motorcycles)
  Level 2 Award in MOT Testing (classes 4 and 7 - light vehicles)
  Level 3 Award in MOT Test Centre Management


Apply for approval

Contact an awarding organisation to find out:


  if you’re eligible to be a training provider
  how to register with them


You can register with more than one.

When you’re approved

You’ll be responsible for approving individual MOT trainers to run the qualification courses.

The awarding organisations you register with will give you more information about running the courses.

Provide annual training

MOT testers must complete training between April and March every year. You can provide training courses for them to take.

MOT testers have to study different topics depending on whether they test:


  class 1 and 2 vehicles (‘group A’)
  class 3, 4, 5, 7 vehicles (‘group B’)


2021 to 2022 topics

These are the topics for 1 May 2021 to 31 March 2022.

Group A

Testers who test vehicles in group A, need to know about:


  new vehicle technology
  MOT test procedures
  MOT testing requirements
  corrosion and standards of repair
  the MOT inspection manual for motorcycles and sidecars



Group B

Testers who test vehicles in group B, need to know about:


  new vehicle technology
  MOT testing requirements
  corrosion and standards of repair
  the MOT inspection manual for cars and passenger vehicles



Groups A and B

MOT testers who test vehicles in both group A and group B need to study all the topics. They will need to train for at least 6 hours a year (instead of 3) and take 2 annual assessments.

Questions on new vehicle technology

MOT testers will be asked questions about:


  lighting systems, multiple function lamps
  electric, hybrid and hydrogen-powered vehicles
  health and safety when dealing with alternative fuel vehicles
  carbon fibre components
  ‘fly-by-wire’ steering systems (group B only)


Questions on MOT test procedures (group A only)

MOT testers who test group A will also be asked questions on test procedures such as:


  raising the machine to check suspension
  testing machines with linked brake systems
  identifying the appropriate headlamp pattern
  repaired lamps
  additional stop lamps
  off-road motorcycles


Questions on MOT testing requirements

MOT testers will be asked questions about:


  DVSA vehicle examiner powers and procedures
  training logs
  recording vehicle details
  the rules on pass after rectification at station (PRS)
  emissions and smoke test
  recording major, minor and dangerous defects
  what to do with technical enquires
  chassis types (group B only)
  test equipment, what to do in the event of failure (group B only)


Questions on MOT inspection manual

These are some examples of the subjects MOT testers will be asked questions about:


  tyres
  vehicles tyre mixed construction
  tyre markings, sidewall instruction (DOR)
  where tread depth is measured
  headlamp conversions
  headlamp aim requirements


These are some examples of the subjects MOT testers might be asked about for group A:


  linked brakes recording results
  brake performance including linked brakes
  multi link suspension movement
  parallel misalignment acceptable limits
  jacking of rear suspension


These are some examples of the subjects MOT testers might be asked about for group B:


  what to do if linked brake test equipment goes down
  basic emissions test (BET) fails
  smoke test
  brake performance
  entering brake results
  information on specific vehicles
  driver’s view
