RAND Economist Kathryn Edwards Available for Interviews on COVID-19


April 2, 2020
The RAND Corporation's Kathryn Edwards is available for interviews on the coronavirus' effect on the economy, whether the U.S. is already in a recession, and the role of unemployment insurance.
Edwards is an associate economist at RAND and a professor at the Pardee RAND Graduate School.
As a labor economist, her research spans science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) education pipeline and labor market; the financial resources available to unemployed households; the challenges in retirement facing older Americans; and the labor market issues for workers without a college degree.
