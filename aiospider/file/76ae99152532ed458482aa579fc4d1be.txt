          Details


          

    
            
This licence allows you to disturb barn owls to survey them by observation to assess what effect a development project could have on them. You can survey for barn owl:


  presence or absence
  distribution


There’s no limit to the number of barn owls that you may disturb, as long as you meet all the terms and conditions in licence CL29 (available on this page).

You must minimise disturbance to barn owls and other species of wild bird and any action which might endanger their breeding success.

You must apply for a permit from the British Trust for Ornithology to do:


  scientific research
  conservation work
  ringing or marking
  nest recording


Register with Natural England

Before you can use this licence, you must register with Natural England using the application form linked on this page.

You need to pay for a CL29 licence.

It costs £60 to register for a new CL29 licence.

From January 2022, it will cost £35 to renew your registration.

Read the terms and conditions for paying for a wildlife licence.

How to pay

You can pay using a bank card. Follow the GOV.UK Pay link on the application form.

How to report your actions

You must report your actions taken using this licence each year, even if you’ve taken no action.

Send your completed report form LR29 to Natural England by 31 January each year using the details on the form.

You’ll need the names of any accredited agents who carried out the surveys on your behalf. 
For each site, you must record:


  survey location (10 figure Ordnance Survey grid reference and what3words

  number of surveys taken
  start and end dates of each survey
  if you did not find barn owl
  the highest number of juveniles or adult barn owls you found


You must also send your results to the Local Record Centre. You can do this by recording your results on the iRecord website or contacting your local record centre.

Licence renewal

By returning your report form LR29 each year you’ve renewed your licence. You should check for any changes to the terms and conditions of use on the latest published version of this licence.
